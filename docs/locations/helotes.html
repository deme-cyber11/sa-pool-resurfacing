Our Process

How Pool Resurfacing Works in Helotes

1

Free Estimate

We visit your Helotes home, inspect the pool including the shell, coping, and deck, and provide a detailed written estimate — no charge, no obligation.

2

Drain Pool

We drain and properly dispose of your pool water in compliance with Bexar County regulations, protecting your Hill Country landscaping throughout the process.

3

Prep Surface

The old surface is removed, the shell inspected for cracks (common in caliche soil areas), structural issues are repaired, and the substrate is prepared for the new finish.

4

Apply Finish

Our experienced crew applies your chosen plaster, quartz, or pebble finish evenly across the entire pool shell, including steps, benches, and the floor.

5

Refill & Balance

We fill the pool and start the water chemistry process using a protocol that accounts for northwest San Antonio's hard water and protects your new finish from scale buildup from day one.

Common Questions

Helotes Pool Resurfacing FAQ

How much does pool resurfacing cost in Helotes?

Pool resurfacing in Helotes typically ranges from $3,500–$5,500 for white plaster, $5,000–$8,500 for quartz aggregate, and $7,000–$12,000 for pebble finishes. If your pool has significant cracking from soil movement — common in caliche-rich areas like Helotes — structural repairs may add to the cost. All repairs are identified upfront in our free estimate.

My Helotes pool has cracking — can you fix it?

Yes. Structural cracking is very common in Helotes due to the caliche and limestone soil that expands and contracts with moisture. We assess all cracks during the free estimate, repair them before applying the new surface, and use flexible bonding systems that accommodate normal soil movement. Many Helotes pools with significant cracking have been successfully resurfaced and have held up beautifully for 10+ years after our work.

When is the best time to resurface a Helotes pool?

The best time to resurface a Helotes pool is late fall through early spring — November through March — when temperatures are milder and you can avoid losing swim season. Spring and fall resurfacing are also fine. We apply surfaces during cooler parts of the day in summer if needed and adjust our process to ensure proper curing even in Texas heat.
