Frequently Asked Questions — Pool Resurfacing in Universal City

How much does pool resurfacing cost in Universal City?

Pool resurfacing in Universal City typically costs $3,500 to $11,000. Standard white plaster runs $3,500 to $5,000, quartz aggregate finishes cost $5,000 to $8,000, and premium pebble options like PebbleTec range from $7,000 to $11,000. Many Universal City pools are mid-sized residential pools from the 1960s through 1980s, which tend to fall in the mid-range. Call for a free estimate.

My Universal City pool is from the 1970s. Can it still be resurfaced?

Yes. Many Universal City pools date to the 1960s, 70s, and 80s when the community was first developed around Randolph AFB. These older pools are excellent candidates for resurfacing as long as the shell structure is sound. We inspect for structural cracks, rebar corrosion, and plumbing condition before recommending a resurfacing plan. Most older Universal City pools can be brought back to like-new condition with a fresh surface.

How long will a new pool surface last in Universal City?

Lifespan depends on the finish you choose and how well the water chemistry is maintained. Standard white plaster lasts 8 to 12 years, quartz aggregate lasts 12 to 18 years, and pebble finishes last 15 to 25 years. Given the hard water and intense sun in the San Antonio area, we typically recommend quartz or pebble for Universal City homeowners who want the best long-term value.
