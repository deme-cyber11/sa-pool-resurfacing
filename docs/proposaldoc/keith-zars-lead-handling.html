Section 1 — End-to-End Lead Flow

From homeowner search to signed contract — every handoff point mapped.

1
Homeowner searches → finds alamopoolresurfacing.com A homeowner in the San Antonio metro searches for pool construction or renovation on Google. Iron Tiger Digital's site ranks organically and appears in results. The homeowner reads the content and decides to reach out — by submitting a contact form or calling the tracked phone number on the site. Organic intent — no paid clicks
2
Lead captured and validated The form submission or call is captured in real time. Iron Tiger Digital validates the lead against the criteria in Section 3 of the Agreement: full name present, working phone number, service address within 50 miles of San Antonio, and a project type that matches the site's scope (new build, renovation, or resurface). Incomplete or out-of-territory submissions are not forwarded. Validation before delivery
3
Lead packet assembled and delivered within 1 hour Iron Tiger Digital compiles a private lead packet containing all captured data and emails it from costa@irontigerleads.com to the designated Keith Zars delivery address. During business hours (Mon–Fri 8 AM–8 PM CT, Sat 10 AM–5 PM CT), delivery happens within 1 hour of submission. After-hours leads arrive by 9 AM CT the next business morning. Delivery SLA: 1 hour (business hours)
4
Keith Zars receives, triages, and calls within 5 minutes The lead arrives exclusively to Keith Zars Pools. Your team reviews the packet, dials the homeowner, and initiates the sales process. Speed-to-lead is the single highest-leverage variable in conversion — the research on this is consistent and clear (see Section 3 of this document). Exclusive — no other contractor receives it
5
Job closes → Keith Zars reports close When a lead converts to a signed contract, reply to the original lead email with the project address and signed date — takes under 30 seconds. This triggers the billing cycle. 30-second report triggers invoice
6
Iron Tiger Digital invoices $500 · Net-15 An invoice is sent within 2 business days of the close report. Payment is due Net-15 by ACH, Stripe, or Zelle. No payment is due for any lead that doesn't close — zero dollars owed on unconverted leads regardless of how many are delivered. Pay only on closed jobs

Section 3 — Speed-to-Lead Protocol

Speed-to-lead is the most controllable variable in your close rate. The data below reflects contractor and home services industry benchmarks — the pattern is consistent regardless of source.

Response Time Likelihood of Contact Effect on Close
< 5 minutes ~9× more likely than 30-min Peak — homeowner still at the device
5 – 30 minutes Acceptable Still strong — call quickly
30 min – 2 hours Contact rate drops sharply Homeowner may have moved on
> 2 hours Significantly degraded Lead goes cold rapidly
After-hours leads Call first thing next morning — ideally within 30 min of 8 AM CT opening. Don't hold until afternoon.
The competing call risk: Iron Tiger Digital's exclusivity covers its own distribution — homeowners may contact other contractors on their own through other channels (Google, referrals, other sites). The faster you call, the more likely you are the first voice they hear. Exclusive delivery gives you the structural advantage; speed-to-lead is how you capitalize on it.

Recommended setup: Route the lead delivery email to a shared inbox (office manager + a salesperson) with a push notification on mobile. The goal is a human eyeball on every lead within 5 minutes of receipt, and a dial within 10.

Section 5 — CRM Intake & Workflow Integration

How to route leads from the delivery email into your existing sales workflow — regardless of which system you use.

Manual intake (any CRM or spreadsheet): When the lead email arrives, create a new contact or deal record using the fields from the packet. Recommended fields to capture:

  • Source tag: "Iron Tiger Digital / Alamo Pool" — separates this channel from referrals, Angi leads, and other sources in your reporting
  • Lead received date + time — critical for close reporting and billing reconciliation
  • Project type (resurface / renovation / new build) — helps your estimator prep before the first call
  • Contract signed date — needed for close reporting; log it at the moment of signature
  • Original lead email thread — keep it accessible; replying to that thread is how you report closes
CRM automation (optional): If your CRM supports email parsing (HubSpot, Salesforce, Jobber, ServiceTitan, etc.), the lead delivery email format is consistent enough to auto-create a contact record on arrival. The packet fields — name, phone, address, project type — are labeled the same way in every email. Ask your CRM admin to set up an inbox rule that creates a new lead record when email arrives from costa@irontigerleads.com. Iron Tiger Digital can provide field labels in a structured format on request.

Recommended pipeline stages for ITD leads: New → Attempted Contact → Appointment Set → Estimate Sent → Closed Won → Closed Lost. Tag all "Closed Won" records with the signed date before replying to report the close.

Section 6 — Close Reporting

Reporting a closed job is how you trigger invoicing. The process is intentionally minimal.

The 30-second report: When a job closes, find the original lead delivery email and reply with two pieces of information: (1) the project address, and (2) the date the contract was signed. That's it. Iron Tiger Digital will send an invoice within 2 business days.
  • How to report
    Reply to the original lead delivery email from costa@irontigerleads.com. Subject line can stay as-is — threading keeps the context. No new email or portal needed.
  • What to include
    Project address (street address of the job) and signed contract date. Optional but appreciated: job type (resurface, renovation, new build) and approximate project value — helps Iron Tiger Digital calibrate lead quality over time, not used for billing.
  • When to report
    At the time the contract is signed. Do not wait until the job is completed or invoiced to the homeowner — the billing trigger is signed contract, not project completion.
  • What if you forget
    Report it when you remember — there's no late-reporting penalty. Iron Tiger Digital does not audit unreported closes. The close-report system is honor-based; the fee structure is designed to make honest reporting the rational choice (you only pay on value received).
  • Multiple closes in one month
    Report each close separately by replying to its respective lead email. This keeps invoices matched to specific leads and simplifies AP reconciliation on your end.

Section 7 — Disputing an Invalid Lead

Iron Tiger Digital validates every lead before delivery, but occasionally a lead slips through that shouldn't count as billable. Here's what qualifies and how to dispute it.

Grounds for a valid dispute:

  • Phone number is not in service or belongs to someone who has no knowledge of the inquiry
  • Service address is outside the 50-mile San Antonio territory
  • Lead is a duplicate — the same homeowner was delivered within the prior 60 days
  • Lead is clearly spam or a bot submission (no real project described, obviously false name)

Not grounds for a dispute:

  • Homeowner doesn't answer repeated calls — this is a follow-up challenge, not an invalid lead
  • Homeowner decides not to move forward after contact — intent was real at submission
  • Homeowner got a quote from another contractor on their own — exclusivity covers Iron Tiger Digital's distribution only
  • Lead didn't close — no payment is owed on unconverted leads regardless of reason

Dispute process:

  1. Reply to the lead email within 5 business days of delivery Flag the issue in the reply: "This lead appears invalid — [reason]." Disputes submitted after 5 business days may not be credited.
  2. Iron Tiger Digital reviews within 2 business days We'll cross-reference the call recording (if applicable), form submission, and lead data. We'll email you the outcome.
  3. Resolution: credit or confirmation If the dispute is valid, the lead is removed from billable count and any invoice adjusted. If the lead meets delivery criteria, we'll explain why and the lead stands. Either way, you get a written response.
  4. Escalation window: 10 business days If you disagree with the outcome, both parties agree to resolve in good faith within 10 business days before pursuing any other remedy — per the Agreement.

Section 8 — Billing & Reconciliation

  • Invoice trigger
    Close report received from Keith Zars (reply to lead email with project address + signed date). Invoices are not issued speculatively or in advance.
  • Invoice timing
    Sent within 2 business days of the close report. Issued electronically — a pay link is included directly in the invoice email.
  • Payment due
    Net-15 from invoice date. Invoices unpaid after 30 days may accrue interest at 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance.
  • Payment methods
    ACH / bank transfer (preferred — no fee, most B2B clients use this) · Stripe (card, click-to-pay from invoice) · Zelle for Business (same-day option)
  • Billing disputes
    If an invoice doesn't match your close records, email costa@irontigerleads.com with the invoice number and the discrepancy. Iron Tiger Digital can provide the signed contract date or invoice number you submitted for any line item. Both parties resolve billing disputes in good faith within 10 business days.
  • Monthly reconciliation
    The shared lead log (available on request) shows every lead delivered with its timestamp. Cross-reference against your CRM's closed deals tagged "Iron Tiger Digital" to verify billing accuracy at month end.

Section 9 — Termination & Transition Handling

How the relationship winds down if either party gives notice — and what stays owed.

  • Notice required
    30 days written notice by email — to costa@irontigerleads.com for notice to Iron Tiger Digital, or to the address on file for notice to Keith Zars Pools.
  • Leads in flight
    Any lead delivered before the termination date remains billable if it closes — even if the close happens after notice is given. The 30-day notice period does not waive payment on leads already in your pipeline.
  • Lead delivery stop
    Iron Tiger Digital stops delivering new leads effective the termination date. No leads are delivered after the notice period expires.
  • Zero-volume exit
    If Iron Tiger Digital's San Antonio lead volume drops to zero for 60 consecutive days, Keith Zars Pools may terminate with written notice and no penalty period — no need to wait out the standard 30 days.
  • Asset ownership
    All websites, domains, and rankings used to generate leads remain Iron Tiger Digital's property. Termination gives Keith Zars Pools no claim to those assets. The site stays live and generating leads — Iron Tiger Digital will re-engage a new contractor in the market.
  • Homeowner data
    Lead data received during the agreement remains confidential per Section 8 of the Agreement, including after termination. Do not sell or distribute homeowner contact information to third parties.
