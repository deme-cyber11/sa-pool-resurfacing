Signed a Deal? Here's How to Tell Us.

One reply to one email. That's what triggers your invoice — nothing else required.

The Close Signal
Reply to the original lead email
When a contract is signed, go back to the lead delivery email from costa@irontigerleads.com and reply with two things:
  1. The project address
  2. The date the contract was signed
Example reply
Subject: Re: Lead — Don Henderson · Pool Resurface · San Antonio

Hey Costa — this one closed.
Address: 4821 Crestview Dr, San Antonio TX 78229
Signed: May 16, 2026
Billing trigger is contract signed, not job completed. Report it the day they sign — not when you invoice the homeowner, not when the job finishes. That reply is what starts the clock on the invoice.

What happens after you reply

1
Costa receives your close report The reply to the lead thread lands in Costa's inbox. No portal, no form — just the email thread you already have.
2
Invoice sent within 2 business days Costa issues an invoice for $500 tied to that specific lead. A payment link is included directly in the email — one click to pay. Invoice within 2 business days
3
Payment due Net-15 ACH (preferred, no fee) · Stripe (click-to-pay) · Zelle for Business. No payment is owed on leads that don't close — ever. Pay only on closed jobs

Billing details

  • Per-close fee
    $500 per closed contract. Flat rate — no percentage, no variable pricing, no retroactive adjustments.
  • Unconverted leads
    Zero owed. A lead that doesn't close is never billed, regardless of how many are delivered or how long they've been in your pipeline.
  • Multiple closes
    Reply separately to each lead's original email thread. One reply per close keeps invoices matched to specific leads and simplifies your AP reconciliation.
  • Late reporting
    No penalty. Report it when you remember. The close-report system is honor-based — the fee structure makes honest reporting the rational choice.
  • Billing question
    Email costa@irontigerleads.com with the invoice number. Both parties resolve billing questions in good faith within 10 business days.
