The One-Liner

When someone asks "what is this?"

💬
Say this:
"We partner with a digital marketing company that ranks a local pool resurfacing website in San Antonio. When homeowners find it and fill out a form or call in, those leads route exclusively to us — no competing contractors, no shared lists. We only pay when a job closes."
How It Works

The mechanics in plain language

🌐
We don't pay for the website
Iron Tiger Digital owns and operates AlamoPools.com. They rank it through SEO — no ads, no PPC. We don't fund the marketing; we just receive the leads it generates.
📞
Leads come to us exclusively
Every form fill and phone call from that site routes directly to Keith Zars. No other pool company in SA gets these leads — it's a single-tenant channel.
💵
$500 per completed job — nothing else
No retainer. No monthly fee. No setup cost. We pay $500 only after a booked job reaches completion. If a lead doesn't close, we owe nothing.
📈
Volume builds over time
The site is already ranking. Early months may be lighter; as rankings strengthen and trust builds, lead volume increases. No ramp-up fees during that period.
The Business Case

Why this makes sense for Keith Zars

🎯
Zero downside risk
Pay-per-completed-job means the channel is self-funding. If we close 10 jobs at $500 each, we spent $5,000 and earned multiples of that in revenue. Worst case: leads trickle slowly. There's no sunk cost.
🔒
No competitor gets these leads
Unlike shared lead services (Angi, HomeAdvisor), this is a single-tenant arrangement. If AlamoPools.com ranks for "pool resurfacing San Antonio," that traffic belongs to Keith Zars.
🤝
We're motivated by the same outcome
Iron Tiger only gets paid when Keith Zars closes a job. Our incentive is to send well-qualified leads and make sure the handoff is smooth. Garbage leads cost us too.
Objection Handling

What they might push back on — and how to answer

"How do we know these are real leads?"
Every lead comes with a name, phone, and email — they reached out themselves. We'll track each one. If a lead is clearly bogus (wrong number, fake name), we don't count it.
"What if the lead volume is too low to matter?"
There's no cost if volume is low — we're not on a retainer. Even 2-3 closed jobs a month at an average ticket of $4,000–$8,000 makes the math work. Volume grows as rankings compound.
"We already get leads from our own marketing."
This is additive, not a replacement. Different channel, different audience. People who find AlamoPools.com may never have searched for "Keith Zars" directly — it expands the top of funnel.
"What's stopping you from sending leads to a competitor later?"
The agreement gives Keith Zars exclusivity in the SA market while the relationship is active. If we ever ended the agreement, there's a notice period — not a sudden switch.
"$500 per job seems high."
Average pool resurfacing job in SA is $4,000–$10,000+. At $500 per close, the cost of acquisition is 5–12% — well below typical paid advertising CAC. And there's no spend until the job is done.
