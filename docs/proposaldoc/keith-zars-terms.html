Lead Generation Service Terms

Iron Tiger Digital  ·  Keith Zars Pools  ·  Effective Date: [Date]  ·  Confidential
This document contains the binding terms for lead generation services between Iron Tiger Digital and Keith Zars Pools. It is intended to be read alongside the accompanying Proposal document. By signing below, both parties agree to all terms herein.
§0 · Pilot Program
30-Day Performance Test — Binding Exit Rights
For the first thirty (30) days following the Effective Date ("Pilot Period"), Provider will deliver up to ten (10) Leads at the standard rate of $500 per Lead. If fewer than three (3) of the first ten (10) Leads result in a Booked Appointment within the Pilot Period, Client may terminate this Agreement in writing with no further obligation — no wind-down fees, no additional invoices for Leads delivered during the Pilot Period.

"Booked Appointment" means a confirmed, scheduled in-person site visit or consultation between Keith Zars Pools personnel and the homeowner who submitted the Lead, occurring within 30 days of Lead delivery.

If three (3) or more Booked Appointments are achieved within the Pilot Period, this Agreement automatically continues on a month-to-month basis under §10. Provider does not guarantee any minimum Lead volume during the Pilot Period or thereafter — Lead volume depends on organic search traffic to Provider's websites.
§1 · Parties & Effective Date
Iron Tiger Digital and Keith Zars Pools
This Lead Generation Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [Date] by and between Iron Tiger Digital, a Texas sole proprietorship (operated by Costa Demetral, costa@irontigerleads.com, (225) 535-4918) ("Provider") and Keith Zars Pools ("Client"). Provider and Client are each referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."
§2 · Lead Definition
What constitutes a billable lead
A "Lead" is an inbound form submission or phone inquiry originated from Provider's lead generation website(s), from a homeowner located within the agreed service territory, expressing genuine interest in new pool construction or major pool renovation services. A valid Lead must include: (a) a full name, (b) a working contact phone number, and (c) a service address within the service territory. Leads that fail to meet these criteria are subject to the dispute process in §7.
§3 · Delivery Process
How leads are delivered
Provider will deliver each Lead to Client via email within one (1) hour of receipt during business hours (8 AM–8 PM CT, Monday–Friday). Each delivery will include, to the extent available: full name, phone number, email address, service address, project description, and submission timestamp. Provider may deliver Leads received outside business hours on the next business morning.

A shared lead delivery log (read-only Google Sheet) is available to Client on request — every Lead, timestamped, with dispute status tracked in real time. For any Lead disputed under §7, Provider will supply the associated call recording within 3 business days of a written request.
§4 · Exclusivity & Intellectual Property
Each lead delivered to Client only — Provider retains all site assets
Once a Lead is delivered to Client, Provider will not sell, transfer, or share that same Lead with any other contractor or third party. Client acknowledges that Leads are sourced from publicly accessible websites and that homeowners may independently contact other contractors; however, Provider's exclusive obligation is to deliver each Lead to Client only and to no other party through Provider's distribution.

All websites, domain names, content, and organic search rankings used to generate Leads remain the sole property of Provider at all times. This Agreement does not transfer, license, or assign any ownership in Provider's websites or digital assets to Client, and termination does not grant Client any right to replicate or acquire those assets.

Upon reasonable written request (no more than once per calendar quarter), Client may request a read-only copy of the lead delivery log to verify exclusivity for any specified date range. Provider will respond within 14 business days.
§5 · Service Territory
San Antonio metro, New Braunfels, Corpus Christi, and surrounding areas
Provider will deliver Leads originating from homeowners located within 100 miles of San Antonio, Texas, which includes but is not limited to: San Antonio metro, New Braunfels, Corpus Christi, Seguin, Boerne, Kerrville, and surrounding communities. Leads from addresses clearly outside this territory are disputable under §7.
§6 · Fees & Payment
$500/lead · Net-15 · Weekly ACH invoice
Client agrees to pay Provider $500 (USD) per delivered Lead. Provider will issue a weekly invoice every Friday covering all Leads delivered during that calendar week. Payment is due Net-15 from the invoice date via ACH bank transfer — routing and account details are provided with the first invoice.

There is no setup fee, no monthly retainer, and no minimum volume commitment. Melio or Bill.com AP automation is available on request at no additional cost to either party. Invoices unpaid after 30 days from the due date may accrue interest at 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance.
§7 · Dispute Process
14 calendar days · Credits only · No cash refunds
Client must raise any dispute within fourteen (14) calendar days of Lead delivery by emailing costa@irontigerleads.com with the Lead name, delivery date, and reason. Eligible dispute reasons: (a) wrong or disconnected phone number, (b) service address clearly outside the defined territory, or (c) exact duplicate of a Lead already delivered and billed in the same calendar month.

If Provider accepts the dispute, a credit equal to $500 is applied to Client's next weekly invoice. No cash refunds. Disputes not raised within 14 calendar days are deemed waived. Disputes based on failed close, homeowner unavailability, or competitive loss are not eligible.

If a dispute is accepted and a credit is issued, Client agrees to delete the disputed Lead data from its records within 30 days and certify deletion in writing upon Provider's request.
§8 · No Conversion Guarantee
Provider delivers intent; Client closes the job
Provider makes no representation or warranty regarding the conversion rate of Leads. Provider's obligation is to deliver qualified inbound Leads meeting the definition in §2. Whether a Lead results in a scheduled appointment, signed contract, or completed job is solely the responsibility of Client's sales and operations team.
§9 · Confidentiality
Lead data stays private
Each Party agrees to keep Lead data (homeowner names, addresses, phone numbers, and project details) confidential and to use it solely for the purposes of this Agreement. Client will not resell, share, or distribute Lead data to any third party. Provider will not disclose Client's lead volume, close rates, or pricing to any competitor. This obligation survives termination.
§10 · Term & Termination
Month-to-month · 30 days written notice · Data return
This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues on a month-to-month basis following the Pilot Period. Either Party may terminate by providing thirty (30) days written notice via email to the other Party's address on file.

Upon termination: (a) Provider will cease delivering Leads, (b) all invoices for Leads delivered prior to termination remain due and payable under the payment terms, (c) any pending disputes will be resolved under the normal process, and (d) Client will cease using all delivered Lead data within 90 days and, upon written request, certify in writing that all Lead data has been deleted. Provider will supply a final CSV export of all Leads delivered during the term within 14 business days of termination. There are no early-termination penalties.
§11 · Limitation of Liability
Provider liability capped at fees paid in prior 30 days
Provider's maximum aggregate liability to Client under this Agreement shall not exceed the total amount of fees actually paid by Client to Provider in the thirty (30) days immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim. In no event shall either Party be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, special, or punitive damages, including but not limited to lost profits, lost revenue, or damage to goodwill, even if advised of the possibility of such damages.
§12 · Force Majeure
Search algorithm changes, outages, and events outside Provider's control
Provider will not be liable for any failure or delay in Lead delivery caused by circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to: search engine algorithm updates, domain deindexation, Google penalties, natural disasters, or internet outages ("Force Majeure Event").

Provider will notify Client within 7 business days of becoming aware of any Force Majeure Event that is materially disrupting Lead delivery volume. If Provider is unable to deliver Leads for more than 30 consecutive days due to a Force Majeure Event, either Party may terminate this Agreement in writing with no further obligation or penalty.
§13 · Governing Law
State of Texas — Bexar County
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to its conflict of law provisions. Any disputes that cannot be resolved informally shall be brought in the state or federal courts located in Bexar County, Texas.
§14 · TCPA Compliance
Consent and telecommunications law
Provider represents that all Leads are generated from opt-in web forms that include written consent language disclosing that the homeowner may be contacted by a licensed pool contractor regarding their inquiry — sufficient to constitute prior express written consent under current TCPA and FCC standards. Provider's lead capture forms include the following or substantially similar disclosure:

"By submitting this form, you agree to be contacted by a licensed pool contractor in your area. You may receive calls, texts, or emails at the information provided. Consent is not a condition of purchase. Reply STOP to opt out of text messages."

Client acknowledges its independent responsibility for complying with all applicable telecommunications laws when contacting Leads, including but not limited to: the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), applicable FCC regulations, the National Do Not Call Registry, restrictions on the use of automated telephone dialing systems (ATDS) or pre-recorded messages, and any applicable state-level telemarketing laws. Client is solely responsible for its own outreach practices, including call frequency, use of autodialers, and honoring all opt-out requests.
