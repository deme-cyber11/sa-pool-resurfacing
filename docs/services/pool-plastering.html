Pool Plastering FAQ

How long does pool plaster last in San Antonio?

White plaster in San Antonio typically lasts 8â€“12 years with proper water chemistry maintenance. The Edwards Aquifer's hard water accelerates calcium scale deposits on plaster, which is why we strongly recommend monitoring calcium hardness levels (ideal: 200â€“400 mg/L) and pH (7.2â€“7.6) throughout the plaster's life. Neglecting chemistry can cut the lifespan to 5â€“7 years.

Should I choose plaster or pebble for my San Antonio pool?

If you're on a budget or planning to sell within 5â€“7 years, plaster is a solid choice. If you plan to keep the pool long-term and want the lowest lifetime cost of ownership, a pebble or quartz aggregate finish is worth the higher upfront investment â€” it lasts twice as long and requires less chemical management in San Antonio's hard water.

Why is my pool plaster turning gray or staining?

Calcium scaling, algae staining, and metal staining (from iron or copper in San Antonio's water) are the most common causes of plaster discoloration. Calcium scale appears as white or grayish patches; algae staining produces green, brown, or black marks; metal staining creates rust or purple-black spots. Many of these can be treated with acid washing without full resurfacing, but severely stained or degraded plaster typically requires a full resurface to restore properly.
