Why Tile Matters

The Importance of Pool Waterline Tile

The waterline tile band around a pool serves two critical functions: it protects the bond beam (the top edge of the pool shell where the plaster meets the coping) from water infiltration, and it provides a washable surface that handles the constant splash zone where oil, sunscreen, and minerals accumulate.

In San Antonio, the Edwards Aquifer's hard water creates thick calcium scale deposits on waterline tile ‚Äî that white, crusty buildup you see at the waterline. While this can be cleaned, repeated cycles of scale buildup eventually loosen tile adhesive and cause tiles to pop off. When this happens, water can penetrate the bond beam and cause much more costly damage.

We also replace coping ‚Äî the capstones around the pool's top edge ‚Äî which takes the brunt of foot traffic, furniture, and UV exposure. Damaged coping allows water into the pool shell structure, which is a leading cause of serious pool damage in the San Antonio area.

Our Process

Pool Tile Repair Process

1

Free Inspection & Tile Selection

We assess the extent of tile and coping damage, identify any underlying bond beam issues, and present tile sample options for you to review. All at no charge.

2

Lower Water Level

For waterline tile work, we lower the pool water level to expose the tile band. For full tile and coping replacement, the pool is drained completely.

3

Remove Old Tile & Prep

Old tile and adhesive are removed from the bond beam. The surface is cleaned, any water damage is repaired, and a waterproof bonding layer is applied before new tile is set.

4

Set New Tile

New tile is set with pool-grade adhesive, ensuring even spacing and a level waterline around the entire pool. Coping stones are set with mortar and sealed appropriately.

5

Grout & Seal

Grout is applied, finished, and sealed. We clean the new tile, refill the pool to proper level, and do a final inspection to ensure everything is level, clean, and water-tight.

Why do my pool tiles keep popping off?

In San Antonio, the most common cause is thermal cycling combined with the bond weakening from hard water calcium scale. The pool tile band goes through extreme temperature swings between cold nights and hot Texas days, which stresses the adhesive. Calcium scale buildup further weakens the bond over time. The permanent fix is removing all loose tile, properly preparing the bond beam, and re-tiling with pool-grade adhesive.
